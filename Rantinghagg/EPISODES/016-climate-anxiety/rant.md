# I Feel Guilty For Breathing

**Episode:** 016  
**Category:** Climate Anxiety  
**Source:** Research  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

I feel guilty for breathing. Apparently my carbon footprint is too big.

And I'm supposed to save the planet. By buying eight-pound reusable straws. While one billionaire's yacht emits more in a day than I will in a lifetime.

But sure. It's about my plastic bag. Not the oil companies. Not the shipping industry. Not the fact that my entire existence is apparently a problem.

I can't have kids. Because of the climate. I can't fly. Because of the climate. I can't eat meat. Because of the climate. I can't buy clothes. Because of fast fashion. I can't buy new clothes. Because of manufacturing emissions.

I can't exist. Basically.

And the recycling. Oh, the recycling. I wash out my yogurt pots. I separate my glass from my plastic from my paper. I feel like a good person.

Then I find out most of it goes to landfill anyway. Because China stopped taking our recycling. Because it's not economically viable. Because the system is broken.

But I'm the one who needs to change. Not the corporations. Not the governments. Me. With my reusable bags. And my guilt.

My nan didn't have climate anxiety. She had a war. And rationing. And she made do. And she mended. And she didn't have a carbon footprint. Because she didn't have a footprint. She had a pair of shoes. And she wore them until they fell apart.

We're not saving the planet. We're performing virtue. While the planet burns.

And I feel guilty. For breathing.
