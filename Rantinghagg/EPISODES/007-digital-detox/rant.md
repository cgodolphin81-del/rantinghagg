# The Digital Detox Journey™

**Episode:** 007  
**Category:** Social Media Culture  
**Source:** Content Calendar Day 4  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

So digital detoxes are a thing now. People are paying actual money — hundreds of pounds — to go to a retreat where someone takes their phone away. Something your mum used to do for free when you were being annoying.

And the first thing they do when they arrive at the no-phone retreat? Take a photo for Instagram. "Day one of my digital detox journey! 🌿✨" That's not a detox, love. That's a holiday with better marketing.

Then they spend three days "reconnecting with nature," which apparently means sitting in a field feeling anxious because they don't know what's happening on Twitter. Is Twitter still there? Has someone said something stupid? Of course someone's said something stupid. Someone's always saying something stupid. That's the entire business model.

Day three they crack. Someone finds a laptop in the office and checks their emails "just quickly." Just quickly. That's how every relapse starts. Nobody ever checks their emails slowly and deliberately.

Then they come home, post a fifteen-slide carousel about their "transformative experience," spend four hours responding to comments about it, and the next day they're doom-scrolling at 2am again.

The detox lasted seventy-two hours. The Instagram content about the detox will last forever.

That's not how detoxing works, love.
