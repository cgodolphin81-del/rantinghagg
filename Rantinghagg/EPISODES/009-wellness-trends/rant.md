# Wellness For People Who Have Money

**Episode:** 009  
**Category:** Wellness Industry  
**Source:** Content Calendar + Research  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

My nan's self-care was a cuppa and having a lie-down. Now it's £120 to sit in a room with a singing bowl while someone whispers at you.

And don't get me started on the "investment" language. "An investment in yourself." No, Karen. It's £80 for a jar of cream that is... moisture. Moisture goes on your face. That's not an investment. That's a transaction.

And the crystals. Oh, the crystals. "This one will align your chakras." "This one will manifest abundance." "This one will help you manifest your next paycheck." Which is fair, because you've spent your entire paycheck on this crystal.

You know what actually aligns your chakras? Not having to think about how much you've spent on self-care. Not having to worry about your rent. Not having to pretend your living room is a boutique hotel.

And the sound baths. "Transformative experience." "Healing frequencies." I don't know, maybe it's the fact that I'm not being asked to like and share. Maybe it's the fact that I'm not being told that my aura is wrong.

But no. We've got "wellness" now. Which is just capitalism with better lighting.

You don't need a crystal. You need a nap. You don't need a sound bath. You need silence. You don't need a £12 juice. You need vegetables. And they're free if you grow them yourself. Or £1.50 at the supermarket.
