# I Don't Own Anything Anymore

**Episode:** 010  
**Category:** Subscription Economy  
**Source:** Content Calendar + Research  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

My nan bought a kettle. It boiled water. She owned it until she died. Revolutionary.

Now? Now your kettle is a subscription. Your car is a subscription. Your music is a subscription. Your movies. Your appliances. Your heating. Even the heated seats in your car — you can't have them unless you pay £15 a month to keep them heated.

I'm not joking. You buy a £50,000 car, and the heated seats are "as a service." You have to subscribe to warmth.

We're renting our lives and calling it convenience. "Oh, you don't want to buy the software? Just subscribe!" "Oh, you don't want to buy the movie? Just subscribe!" "Oh, you don't want to own your furniture? Just subscribe!"

And the worst part? You forget to cancel. You're trying to cancel your gym membership, your streaming service, your phone plan, your heating subscription, your car subscription. And somewhere, a company is charging you £300 for something you don't even use.

"Forgot to cancel" is now a lifestyle.

And we're okay with this. We're okay with not owning anything. We're okay with our lives being a series of monthly payments that can be cancelled at any time — by them.

Your nan bought a kettle. She owned it. It boiled water. It was hers. She didn't have to pay £5 a month to feel the warmth.

We're not living in convenience anymore. We're living in rent.
