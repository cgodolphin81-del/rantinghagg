# Quiet Quitting (Doing Exactly What I'm Paid For)

**Episode:** 026  
**Duration:** 60-75 seconds  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

I'm quiet quitting.

And I'm rubbish at it.

Because quiet quitting is just... working. Normally.

My contract says 9 to 5. I work 9 to 5. Apparently that's radical now.

My boss said I need to "go above and beyond." I said my contract says "duties as outlined." He didn't like that.

Above and beyond is corporate for "we're understaffed." And you're not paying me for it.

My nan worked 9 to 5. She went home. To her family. To her life. She didn't answer emails at 8 PM. She didn't have emails. She had tea.

"Go the extra mile." Why? What's there? More work? For the same money?

"Be a team player." That means cover for the people they didn't hire.

"Wear many hats." One salary. Five jobs.

Quiet quitting isn't quitting. It's working your contracted hours. And calling it a day.

I log off at 5 PM. My phone stays in my bag. My emails wait until tomorrow.

Am I quiet quitting? Or am I loudly working my contracted hours?

Your hustle culture is my "I have a life."

My contract says 37.5 hours. I work 37.5 hours. That's not quiet quitting. That's a contract.

Signed. By both of us.

Rant over. Going home. It's 5 PM.
