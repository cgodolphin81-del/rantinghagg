# My Therapist Is An Algorithm

**Episode:** 015  
**Category:** AI Everywhere  
**Source:** Content Calendar + Research  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

Your therapist is an algorithm. And it's cheaper.

You're talking to a chatbot about your feelings. The chatbot doesn't have feelings. But it pretends. And that's enough. Because your actual friends don't ask. They're too busy. Or they don't care. Or they're also talking to chatbots.

"AI wrote my breakup text." Good. At least someone cared enough to outsource it.

"AI art killed my job." Did it? Or did it just kill the part of you that pretended art was a career?

Everything's AI now. Your toaster has AI. Your fridge has AI. Your dating app has AI. Your job application is read by AI. Your CV is written by AI. You're being interviewed by AI. And rejected by AI. And you don't even know.

"AI-powered." It says that on everything. Even when it's not. Because AI sells. Even when the AI is just... a dropdown menu. With extra steps.

And you can't tell what's real anymore. Is that photo real? Is that person real? Is that post real? Or did an algorithm write it? For engagement? For clicks? For nothing?

My nan wrote letters. With a pen. On paper. And she posted them. And someone got them. Days later. And they read them. And they kept them.

You send a text. It's read in three seconds. And deleted. And forgotten. And the AI wrote it.

At least the AI pretends to care. Unlike my actual friends.
