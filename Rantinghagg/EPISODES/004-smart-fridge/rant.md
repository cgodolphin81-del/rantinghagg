# The Smart Fridge

**Episode:** 004  
**Category:** Tech Dependency  
**Source:** Content Calendar Day 1  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

Right, so. Someone — an actual engineer, with a degree and everything — has invented a smart fridge. A fridge that sends you a text message when you're running low on milk.

We used to just… open the fridge. Have a look. "Oh, we're low on milk." Done. Three seconds. No Wi-Fi required.

But no. Now your fridge needs a software update. Your FRIDGE. The thing that keeps cheese cold needs to download a patch. And if your Wi-Fi goes down, what happens? Does the fridge forget what's in it? Does it have an existential crisis? "I don't know who I am anymore. Am I a fridge? Am I a cupboard? I've lost connection to the cloud."

Meanwhile, my nan had a fridge for 40 years. No screen, no app, no Bluetooth. You opened it, you looked inside, you closed it. Revolutionary technology.

And here's the thing — the smart fridge costs three grand. Three thousand pounds. For a fridge that does exactly what the old one did, but also spies on your eating habits. "You've opened the cheese drawer seven times today." Mind your business, fridge.

We put all our effort into making fridges intelligent and somehow made ourselves thicker in the process.

*[sips tea]*
