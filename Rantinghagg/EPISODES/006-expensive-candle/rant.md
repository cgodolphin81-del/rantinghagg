# The Expensive Candle

**Episode:** 006  
**Category:** First World Problems  
**Source:** Content Calendar Day 3  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

Right. Forty-seven pounds. For a candle. Not a chandelier. Not a lamp. A candle. A bit of wax with a string in it.

And it doesn't even smell like something specific. It smells like "Autumn Nostalgia." What does that mean? That's not a smell, that's a feeling. You can't put a feeling in a jar. I've tried. It's called therapy.

You know what autumn actually smells like? Damp leaves. Slightly mouldy conkers. That weird smell when you turn the heating on for the first time and it burns off six months of dust. THAT'S autumn nostalgia. And it's free. Go outside.

But no. We've got "hand-poured" candles now. As opposed to what? Foot-poured? Machine-gunned into the jar? Of course it's hand-poured, Karen. That's how pouring works.

"Made with sustainable soy wax and essential oils." It's a candle. It burns. That's literally the opposite of sustainable. You're setting it on fire on purpose.

And the best bit? It burns for thirty hours. So you're paying forty-seven quid for thirty hours of pretending your living room is a boutique hotel. That's £1.57 an hour to smell like someone else's idea of a memory.

My nan had a candle. It was white. It cost 40p. It smelled like candle. And we were grateful.
