# My Parents' House Cost £30k

**Episode:** 014  
**Category:** Housing Crisis  
**Source:** Content Calendar + Research  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

My parents bought a house. For less than my student debt.

Thirty thousand pounds. For a three-bedroom semi. With a garden. And they paid it off. In ten years. Because that's what you did. You got a job. You got a mortgage. You paid it off.

You need a hundred thousand pounds. For a deposit. On a shoebox. With no garden. And the mortgage is four times your salary. Which you don't have. Because you're paying rent. Which is sixty percent of your income.

And they tell you to stop buying avocado toast.

Avocado toast. That's the problem. Not the fact that house prices have gone up by one thousand percent. Not the fact that wages have stayed the same. Not the fact that your parents' generation bought council houses for pennies and sold them for millions.

No. It's the avocado.

My nan rented a council flat. For the rest of her life. She didn't own. She didn't need to. Rent was cheap. Rent was secure. Rent was... housing. Not an investment vehicle. Not a way for your landlord to buy his third Buy-to-Let.

You're paying your landlord's mortgage. And his holiday. And his second car. And he's calling you "lucky" to have the place.

You don't need avocado advice. You need a time machine. Or a revolution. Whichever comes first.
