# 500 Followers, No Friends

**Episode:** 017  
**Category:** Loneliness Epidemic  
**Source:** Research  
**Status:** Ready for script

---

I have five hundred Instagram followers. And no one to call when I'm having a breakdown.

That's the loneliness epidemic. Right there.

You have 500 followers. 1000 likes. 50 comments. And you're eating dinner alone. Again. Because everyone's "too busy." Or they're also alone. Eating dinner alone. Scrolling through pictures of other people not being alone.

We used to have pubs. Proper pubs. Where you'd go after work. And you'd see the same faces. And you'd have a pint. And you'd talk. About nothing. About everything.

Now we have DMs. And "we should catch up soon!" Which means nothing. It means "I see you. I acknowledge you. But I'm not going to see you."

And making friends as an adult? It's like dating. But worse. Because at least with dating you know what's happening. With friendship? You're just... putting yourself out there. And hoping. And being rejected. But it's not called rejection. It's called "they're busy."

Work from home. Which is great. Until you realise you haven't spoken to another human in three days. Except the delivery driver. And that's the highlight of your week.

Third places are dead. Pubs are closing. Cafes are too expensive. Community centres are... what? Where do you go? Where do you exist? Where do you just... be?

My nan had friends. Proper friends. From work. From the neighborhood. From the WI. She'd see them every week. She'd have a cuppa. She'd have a natter. She'd never be alone.

I have 500 followers. And I'm so lonely I'm starting to talk to my plants. And they're not great conversationalists. They just... sit there. Judging me. For not watering them enough.

We're the most connected generation. And the loneliest.
