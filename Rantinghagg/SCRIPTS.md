# RantingHagg — 15 TikTok/Reels Scripts

> Each script: Hook (first 3 seconds), Build, Punchline, CTA, plus delivery notes.
> Designed for 60-90 seconds. Universally relatable. No targets, just human silliness.

## Script 1: "The Notification Addiction"

**HOOK** *(0-3 seconds)*
*[Deadpan to camera, glasses on nose]*
"My phone buzzed fourteen times during breakfast. I have no friends. What is happening."

**BUILD**
*[Slight lean forward, bewildered]*
I checked. Three apps want me to "come back." Two shops are having a sale I didn't ask about. One wants me to rate a purchase from six months ago. Yes, I liked the tin opener. I liked it SIX MONTHS AGO. Why are you asking me now? Are you insecure? Do you need reassurance? It's a tin opener. It opens tins. Five stars. Move on.

*[Gestures with tea cup]*
Then there's the "someone you might know is on Instagram" notification. I don't want to know them. I actively chose not to know them. That's why I don't follow them. We met once at a barbecue in 2014. That doesn't make us friends, that makes us people who were both near sausages.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Deadpan pause, sip tea]*
My phone has more to say to me than anyone in my actual life. And most of what it says is "SALE ENDS TONIGHT." It's been ending tonight for three weeks.

**CTA**
*[Raise eyebrow]*
"Tag someone whose phone never shuts up. Follow for daily rants."

## Script 2: "The Supermarket Self-Service Lie"

**HOOK**
*[Exasperated expression]*
"Ten items or less? I've been here forty-five minutes."

**BUILD**
*[Counting on fingers]*
The self-service till has four moods: working, broken, screaming about a bagging area, and that weird one where it just goes quiet and you don't know if it's thinking or if it's died.

*[Mimics looking around desperately]*
And there's always one person who brings a full weekly shop to the self-checkout. Forty items. A watermelon. Loose mushrooms they need to WEIGH. Reduced items that won't scan. And they're paying with a combination of cash, card, and coupons from 1997.

*[Deadpan]*
Meanwhile I'm standing behind them holding a pint of milk, watching my youth pass before my eyes.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Flat tone]*
They called it "express." That word used to mean something.

**CTA**
"If this is your life every Tuesday, follow. Misery loves company."

## Script 3: "Why Is Everything 'A Journey' Now?"

**HOOK**
*[Looks directly at camera, puzzled]*
"When did buying a sofa become 'a journey'?"

**BUILD**
*[Theatrical disbelief]*
Everything's a journey now. Weight loss journey. Hair journey. Skincare journey. Sourdough journey.

*[Leans in]*
It's bread, Steven. You made bread. You didn't traverse the Andes. You mixed flour and water and waited. That's not a journey, that's patience.

"My decluttering journey." You put things in bags and took them to a charity shop. That's an errand. That's a fifteen-minute errand. Columbus had a journey. You reorganised a cupboard.

*[Sips tea thoughtfully]*
"My self-love journey." Wonderful. Important, even. But when it involves buying seven hundred pounds worth of products to love yourself with, I'm not sure that's self-love. I think that's shopping.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Pause, deadpan]*
A journey used to require a suitcase and a passport. Now it requires a ring light and a hashtag.

**CTA**
"What's YOUR journey? Tell me in the comments. Make it ridiculous."

## Script 4: "The Nonsense of Hotel Pillows"

**HOOK**
*[Arms crossed]*
"I went to a hotel. There were nine pillows on the bed. NINE. I have one head."

**BUILD**
*[Picks up invisible pillows one by one]*
Nine pillows. Arranged in rows. Different sizes. Different firmness levels. Some square, some rectangular, one that was basically a small mattress. There was a cylindrical one — a TUBE of pillow — just lying there at the front like a fabric sausage dog.

*[Overwhelmed gesture]*
Where do they GO when you sleep? I spent ten minutes removing pillows from the bed and putting them on the floor. Then the floor looked like a pillow shop had exploded. Then I felt bad for the pillows on the floor. They looked sad. I nearly put them back.

And in the morning the cleaning person comes in and puts all nine back. In formation. Every day. Nine pillows, arranged with military precision, for one person who sleeps on one.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Deadpan]*
We can't house everyone but we've given pillows their own postcode.

**CTA**
"Follow if you've ever fought a decorative cushion."

## Script 5: "The 'Seen' Spiral"

**HOOK**
*[Wide eyes, mock horror]*
"They opened my message. They read it. And then… nothing. They chose VIOLENCE."

**BUILD**
*[Pacing slightly]*
I sent a message. A nice message. A perfectly normal message. Something like "are we still on for Saturday?" A yes or no question. Binary. Two options. Not a riddle. Not a philosophical conundrum.

*[Looks at imaginary phone]*
Read. 3:47pm. It is now 6:12pm. That's two hours and twenty-five minutes of silence. Two hours to type "yes." One word. Three letters. The thumb movement alone takes half a second.

*[Gets increasingly theatrical]*
Are they composing an essay? Are they consulting a lawyer? Have they fallen down a well? Should I be worried or offended? These are the questions that now occupy my Tuesday evening instead of anything useful.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Sits down, exhausted]*
In the 1800s, you'd send a letter and wait six weeks for a reply and nobody had a panic attack. Now I'm three hours into a mental health crisis because Craig hasn't confirmed pub.

**CTA**
"Double tap if Craig needs to text back. Follow for more rants."

## Script 6: "The Absurdity of Water"

**HOOK**
*[Holds up imaginary bottle]*
"We're buying WATER now. Buying it. In bottles. Water."

**BUILD**
*[Genuinely baffled]*
Water comes out of a tap. In your house. Basically free. Unlimited supply. Tested, treated, perfectly safe. It comes out of a TAP.

And yet — YET — we walk into a shop and pay two pounds for the same thing in a plastic bottle. Often more than two pounds. I saw water for four quid the other day. Four pounds. For water. The thing that falls from the sky for free.

*[Leaning in]*
And now there's FANCY water. Water with "added electrolytes." Water that's "pH balanced." Water that's been "filtered through volcanic rock." It's water. WATER. The molecule hasn't changed since the dinosaurs drank it. It's two hydrogens and an oxygen. It doesn't need a marketing team.

*[Shakes head]*
And "sparkling" water. Water with bubbles. Angry water, I call it. Nobody actually LIKES sparkling water. They just pretend to because it comes in a glass bottle and makes them feel European.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Flat]*
We've monetised rain. As a species, we looked at a puddle and thought, "How can we charge for this?" And it worked.

**CTA**
"Follow for daily proof that we're ridiculous."

## Script 7: "The Meeting That Should've Been an Email"

**HOOK**
*[Slumps in chair]*
"I've just been in a meeting. An hour. Sixty minutes. Could've been two sentences in an email."

**BUILD**
*[Miming sitting in a meeting]*
The meeting started ten minutes late because Graham couldn't find the link. Graham can never find the link. Graham has attended three hundred virtual meetings and treats every single one like it's his first encounter with technology. "Is it Teams or Zoom?" It's in the calendar invite, Graham. The one you replied to.

*[Continues]*
Then we did introductions. We all know each other. We've worked together for four years. But every meeting starts with "should we go around and say who we are and what we do?" I'm Janet. I do the same thing I did yesterday. And the day before. My role has not changed since the last meeting, which was TUESDAY.

*[Getting more animated]*
Then someone shared their screen and we all watched them try to find the right tab for eight minutes. Eight minutes of "oh, not that one, hang on, where's it gone, oh it's minimised—" JUST SEND THE DOCUMENT.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Looks at camera]*
The answer, by the way, was "yes." The entire meeting was to decide something that could have been answered with the word "yes." In an email.

**CTA**
"Tag someone who books unnecessary meetings. You know who they are."

## Script 8: "The Gym in January"

**HOOK**
*[Knowing look]*
"It's January. The gym is full. Give it three weeks."

**BUILD**
*[Warming to theme]*
Every year. Without fail. January the first, the gym is packed. Every treadmill. Every bike. Every mat. People who haven't exercised since the last January the first have arrived in brand new trainers with that look in their eyes. That "new year, new me" look. That look of determination that will last exactly until January the nineteenth.

*[Counting]*
Week one: full. Car park overflowing. Can't get a locker. Someone's in your spot. You didn't know you had a spot but you DO and they're IN IT.

Week two: slightly less full. A few have already gone. Quietly. Nobody mentions them. Like a herd thinning naturally.

Week three: half empty. The regulars are reclaiming their territory. The staff look relieved.

*[Satisfied nod]*
February: back to normal. The new trainers are in a cupboard. The gym membership is still being charged monthly but nobody talks about that. It's a donation. A charitable donation to a building you visited three times.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Sips tea]*
The gym industry's entire business model is based on optimism in January and guilt in February. And it works. Every single year.

**CTA**
"Follow. I'll still be here in February."

## Script 9: "Why Do We Clap When the Plane Lands?"

**HOOK**
*[Bewildered face]*
"The plane landed. People clapped. CLAPPED. As if it might not have."

**BUILD**
*[Gesturing]*
We're sitting in a metal tube, thirty-seven thousand feet in the air, travelling at five hundred miles an hour, and when it lands — which is, by the way, the EXPECTED outcome, the thing it's designed to do, the thing you PAID for — people clap.

*[Claps sarcastically]*
Well done. You did your job. The pilot landed the plane. Isn't that... the minimum? If a taxi driver got you home safely, would you give them a standing ovation? "Incredible. He used the brakes AND the steering wheel. Masterful."

*[Thoughtful pause]*
But also — and I've been thinking about this — maybe they're not clapping for the pilot. Maybe they're clapping for themselves. For surviving. For getting through three hours next to a man who took his shoes off and ate an egg sandwich.

*[Sniffs]*
Because THAT is an achievement. Sitting in a middle seat for three hours without committing a crime is an achievement that deserves recognition.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Nods slowly]*
So fine. Clap. Clap for survival. Clap because you made it through seat 34B without incident. You've earned it.

**CTA**
"Clap emoji in the comments if you've survived economy class. Follow for more."

## Script 10: "The Out-of-Office Email"

**HOOK**
*[Reading from imaginary screen]*
"I am currently out of the office with limited access to email." Limited. Not NO access. Limited. They're still checking. On holiday."

**BUILD**
*[Theatrical reading]*
The out-of-office reply. A tiny autobiography nobody asked for. "I am currently out of the office from the 14th to the 21st." Fine. Good. Enjoy yourself.

"For urgent matters, please contact—" Nobody's going to contact them. Whatever you think is urgent isn't. The building isn't on fire. Dave can wait.

*[Continues reading]*
But some people — oh, some people — turn their out-of-office into a NOVEL. "I am currently recharging my batteries on the beautiful shores of Sardinia 🌊☀️ Taking time to reconnect with nature and nourish my soul."

*[Looks at camera]*
Brenda. It's an auto-reply. Not a blog post. I emailed you about the printer. I don't need your holiday itinerary.

And "limited access to email" — that means they're on the beach in Sardinia CHECKING THEIR EMAILS. On holiday. Checking emails. That's not recharging, Brenda. That's a geographical change with the same anxiety.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Deadpan]*
A proper holiday is when nobody can reach you and you don't care. Everything else is just working in a nicer chair.

**CTA**
"Follow. I never check my email. Ever."

## Script 11: "The Complicated Coffee Order"

**HOOK**
*[Staring in disbelief]*
"The woman in front of me just ordered a 'half-caff oat milk caramel macchiato with an extra shot and light foam.' That's not a coffee. That's a spell."

**BUILD**
*[Counting ingredients]*
Nineteen words. NINETEEN WORDS to order a hot drink. I counted. There was a time when you walked into a café and said "coffee, please" and they gave you coffee. Brown liquid. Hot. In a cup. Done.

Now you need a degree in linguistics just to order a beverage. Tall, grande, venti. Those are just small, medium, large. We already HAD words for those. They worked fine. They'd worked for centuries.

*[Mimics ordering]*
"Can I get a venti iced skinny hazelnut macchiato with extra shot, light ice, no whip, with oat milk — oh wait, do you have the Oatly or the other one? The Oatly, please — and could you do half the pumps of syrup?"

*[Turns to camera]*
The barista is twenty-two years old and has memorised four hundred drink combinations. This person has more knowledge in their head than most surgeons. And they're paid minimum wage to spell your name wrong on a cup.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Holds up imaginary mug]*
I drink tea. Bag in. Water on. Milk. Done. Four words. Same every time. Never lets me down.

**CTA**
"What's the most ridiculous coffee order you've heard? Comments. Go."

## Script 12: "The 'Live, Laugh, Love' Sign"

**HOOK**
*[Staring at wall]*
"There's a sign in this house that says 'Live, Laugh, Love.' As if we needed instructions."

**BUILD**
*[Walking through imaginary house]*
When did we start putting motivational commands on our walls? Like we might forget? "Oh right, LIVE. I was just going to stop. Thanks, wall. Good reminder."

*[Points at imaginary signs]*
"Eat." Yes, I was going to. "Sleep." Already a fan. "Be kind." I WAS being kind until I saw this sign and it made me unreasonably angry.

*[Picks up imaginary item]*
And it's not just signs. It's cushions. Mugs. Doormats. Tea towels. Your TEA TOWEL says "Good Vibes Only." It's a tea towel. It dries dishes. It doesn't need a philosophy.

There's a doormat that says "Hope You Brought Wine." Oh, wonderful. Alcoholism as décor. How whimsical.

*[Deadpan]*
And "This Is My Happy Place" above the toilet. Is it, Debbie? Is the toilet your happy place? Because if it is, I think we need to talk about something else entirely.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Sits, sips tea]*
My house has one sign. It says "NO SOLICITING." And it actually serves a purpose.

**CTA**
"What's the worst motivational sign you've seen? I need to know. Follow."

## Script 13: "The Parking Space Standoff"

**HOOK**
*[Gripping imaginary steering wheel]*
"Two adults are about to fight to the death over a parking space twenty metres from the door. There's an empty one thirty metres away. They can both see it."

**BUILD**
*[Narrating like a nature documentary]*
Observe. The suburban car park. Two vehicles have spotted the same space. Both indicate. Neither moves. This is a standoff. A battle of wills. A duel without swords.

*[Gets more intense]*
The first driver was clearly here first. They've been waiting. Indicator on. Patient. Reasonable. The second driver has swooped in from the other direction with the energy of someone who believes the universe owes them a parking space.

And now they stare at each other. Through windscreens. For an eternity. Neither will back down. It's not about the space anymore. It's about PRINCIPLE. It's about justice. It's about winning.

*[Gestures to the side]*
Meanwhile — MEANWHILE — there is an empty space. Right there. Thirty metres away. Maybe forty. An extra twenty-second walk. But neither of them will take it because that would mean LOSING. And losing a parking space battle is apparently worse than being late.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Deadpan to camera]*
We conquered fire. We built cities. And this is what we've become. Two mammals in metal boxes having a staring contest over tarmac.

**CTA**
"You've been in this standoff. I know you have. Follow."

## Script 14: "Why Does Everything Have a Podcast?"

**HOOK**
*[Scrolling on imaginary phone]*
"Everyone's got a podcast. Everyone. My plumber's got a podcast."

**BUILD**
*[Overwhelmed]*
There are four million podcasts. Four MILLION. There are not four million interesting things. There are maybe forty interesting things. And they've each got a hundred thousand podcasts about them.

*[Mimics podcast voice]*
"Welcome to my podcast where two friends sit in a room and have a conversation." That's not a podcast. That's a chat. Chats have existed for all of human history. You've just put a microphone in front of one and called it content.

And the names. "Unfiltered." "Raw." "No Holds Barred." "Real Talk." It's two blokes talking about football for ninety minutes. That's not unfiltered, that's a pub. Go to a pub. It's cheaper and you get a pint.

*[Getting animated]*
Everyone thinks they have something to say that needs broadcasting. They don't. Most of what most people say is "did you see that thing" and "I'm a bit tired today." That's not broadcastable. That's Tuesday.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Sips tea]*
I don't have a podcast. I have opinions. There's a difference. One requires equipment. The other just requires tea.

**CTA**
"Don't start a podcast. Follow me instead."

## Script 15: "The Blanket on the Sofa"

**HOOK**
*[Cosy, wrapped in imaginary blanket]*
"There is a blanket on every sofa in Britain. We have heating. We have walls. Why the blanket?"

**BUILD**
*[Looking around]*
Every home. EVERY home. Has a decorative blanket draped artfully over the sofa. Not because it's cold. The heating's on. The windows are double-glazed. Nobody is at risk of hypothermia. But there it is. The blanket.

*[Adjusts imaginary blanket]*
And it's not a real blanket. Not a proper duvet-thick, nan-knitted, been-in-the-family-for-decades blanket. It's a THROW. A thin, overpriced rectangle that keeps nothing warm but looks nice in photos.

*[Gestures]*
You can't actually use it. If you try to use it, someone says "that's the NICE throw." The nice throw. As opposed to what? The naughty throw? Is there a hierarchy of blankets? Is this one for display purposes only, like the good china we never eat off?

And yet — AND YET — the moment you sit on that sofa and pull that useless, decorative, not-warm-enough throw over your legs, something happens. Peace. Comfort. Joy. You are now a human burrito and all is well with the world.

**PUNCHLINE**
*[Snuggles into imaginary throw]*
The blanket serves no practical purpose. But it serves every emotional purpose. And maybe that's the most human thing of all.

**CTA**
*[Warm smile]*
"Follow for more rants. And go get your blanket. You deserve it."
