Stop Buying These 5-Step Success Books

March 24, 2026 / By The Hagg

I went to a bookstore today. It was a mistake. I ended up in the "Self-Help" section, which is basically a 5,000-square-foot monument to human insecurity. Every book has a title like The Zen of Zero Effs or How to Manifest Your First Billion by Thinking Really Hard. And I'm sick of it.

These books are all the same. They're written by a guy named "Chad" who retired at 22 after "grinding" for three weeks. If you look closely at Chad's story, Step 1 is always: "My dad gave me a small loan of $5 million and a Porsche." Well, thanks, Chad. I'll get right on that as soon as I finish my Shift at the coffee shop.

Then there's the "Morning Routine" obsession. You have to wake up at 4:00 AM, drink a smoothie made of kale and sadness, meditate for two hours, and then write a list of 100 things you're grateful for before you even look at a screen. By 8:00 AM, you're so exhausted from "becoming your best self" that you have to take a nap for the rest of the day.

"SPOILER ALERT: STEP 1 IS ALWAYS HAVING $10 MILLION IN THE BANK."

It's professional gaslighting. If you're not successful, it's because you didn't "want it enough." It's because you didn't "vibrate at the right frequency." No, maybe it's because the economy is a dumpster fire and my rent is 90% of my income. But no, Chad says I just need to "re-wire my brain."

Here's my 1-Step Success Guide: Stop buying books written by people who make money by selling books about how to make money. It's an infinite loop of nonsense. Go buy a fiction book. Go read a comic. Go outside and yell at a cloud. It's more productive than "manifesting" a Ferrari while you're eating instant noodles.

Rant over. Go be mediocre. It's fine.

RANT OVER.
